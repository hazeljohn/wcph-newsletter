COMMUNITY SHARE AGRICULTURE (CSA) PROGRAMS:

by: Christian Mackey, M1

Michigan is one of the primary agricultural states in our country and here in Washtenaw County we have a booming local agriculture community where many farms and collectives host their own Community Supported Agriculture(CSA) programs.

What is a CSA and why should I care?


Members pay upfront for the goods produced by farmers available for them as they're grown through the season. This system allows small operations to get the funds needed to start planting while allowing customers to connect and get to know their local producers. Many of you may be familiar with this model, others of you may be hearing about it for the first time. If you're interested in knowing more about CSA options in your area, we've compiled a list of programs in the Ann Arbor area, many of which run out of the Ann Arbor Farmer's Market. UPDATE ON PROJECT DRAWDOWN:

by: Casey Patnode, M3

In this newsletter, we aim to include summaries from portions of Project Drawdown, a comprehensive plan with 100 total strategies to combat climate change. It is based on research from leading scientists and policymakers around the world, and ranked based on their expected impact if implemented. For the first summary, we wanted to introduce one that everyone can participate in and is still extremely impactful, implementing a plant-rich diet. While it may seem simple, it is ranked as having the 4th highest potential impact of all listed strategies!


A 2016 study from the University of Oxford showed that a worldwide transition to plant-based diets could reduce emissions by as much as 70% through vegan diets (and 63% for vegetarian diets) by 2050. This would be in addition to a reduction in global mortality of 6-10% as well as trillions of dollars in savings. One common misconception is that animal meat is required to meet protein requirements, however where plant-based protein is abundant, people do not need animal protein for its nutrients (other than vitamin B12, not present in strict vegan diets).


This is not to say you need to adopt a strict vegan or vegetarian diet to contribute! (Although congratulations to you if you decide to make that change!) By practicing “reducetarianism” and making meat a delicacy, rather than a staple, you can help to combat climate change while getting healthier. Zen master Thich Nhat Hanh once said that making the transition to a plant-based diet may well be the most effect way an individual can stop climate change. The research suggests he is right.
